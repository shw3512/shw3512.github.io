\section{Exercise}

% Reference : SHW

(4 pt) Take as given the following ``infinite polynomial'' expression for $\cos x$:
\begin{align}
\cos x
&=
1 - \frac{1}{2} x^{2} + \frac{1}{24} x^{4} - \frac{1}{720} x^{6} + \frac{1}{40320} x^{8} - \ldots%
\nonumber
\\
&=
1 - \frac{1}{2} x^{2} + \frac{1}{24} x^{4} + O(x^{6})%
\label{eq : LQ11 cos x Taylor Series}
\end{align}
(Recall that $O(x^{6})$ means ``terms involving $x$ to powers $6$ and higher''.) Consider the limit
\begin{align}
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1 - \cos x - \frac{1}{2} x^{2}}{x^{4}}%
\label{eq : LQ11 Limit}
\end{align}

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item\label{itm : LQ11a} (2 pt) Compute the limit in \eqref{eq : LQ11 Limit} by substituting \eqref{eq : LQ11 cos x Taylor Series} for $\cos x$, simplifying, and evaluating.
\end{enumerate}

\spaceSolution{3in}{% Begin solution.
Substituting \eqref{eq : LQ11 cos x Taylor Series} for $\cos x$ in the limit in \eqref{eq : LQ11 Limit}, we get
\begin{align*}
&\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1 - \left(1 - \frac{1}{2} x^{2} + \frac{1}{24} x^{4} + O(x^{6})\right) - \frac{1}{2} x^{2}}{x^{4}}
\\
&=
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1 - 1 + \frac{1}{2} x^{2} - \frac{1}{24} x^{4} - O(x^{6}) - \frac{1}{2} x^{2}}{x^{4}}
\\
&=
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{-\frac{1}{24} x^{4} - O(x^{6})}{x^{4}}
\\
&=
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \left[-\frac{1}{24} - O(x^{2})\right]
\\
&=
-\frac{1}{24}
\end{align*}
In the penultimate (next-to-last) equality we have canceled $x^{4}$ from each term in the numerator. In particular, every term in $O(x^{6})$ involves $x$ to the power $6$ or higher. When we divide these terms by $x^{4}$, every term now involves $x$ to the power $6 - 4 = 2$ or higher. Hence we can collect these terms into what we call $O(x^{2})$.

For the final equality, every term in $O(x^{2})$ involves $x$ to the power $2$ or higher, so direct evaluation of the limit (as $x$ approaches $0$) makes all these terms $0$, leaving only $-\frac{1}{24}$.}% End solution.



\begin{enumerate}[resume,label=(\alph*)]
\item\label{itm : LQ11b} (2 pt) Compute the limit in \eqref{eq : LQ11 Limit} by iteratively applying l'H\^{o}pital's rule. (You should apply l'H\^{o}pital's rule four times. Briefly show it applies each time you use it!) Confirm you get the same result you got in part \ref{itm : LQ11a}.
\end{enumerate}

\spaceSolution{3in}{% Begin solution.
For each equality below, we (i) can check that direct evaluation of the limit on the left gives $\frac{0}{0}$, an indeterminate form to which l'H\^{o}pital's rule applies; and (ii) apply l'H\^{o}pital's rule to get the limit on the right.
\begin{align*}
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1 - \cos x - \frac{1}{2} x^{2}}{x^{4}}
=
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin x - x}{4 x^{3}}
=
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\cos x - 1}{12 x^{2}}
=
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{-\sin x}{24 x}
=
\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{-\cos x}{24}
\end{align*}
Direct evaluation of this last limit gives $-\frac{1}{24}$. This agrees with our answer to part \ref{itm : LQ11a}.}% End solution.
