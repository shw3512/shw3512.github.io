\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item\label{itm : LQ09B a} (2 pt) Show that $\displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1 - \cos(x^{2})}{x^{4}} = \frac{1}{2}$.

\fontHint{While not required, you may use the Taylor series $\cos \theta = 1 - \frac{1}{2} \theta^{2} + \frac{1}{24} \theta^{4} - \ldots$}
\end{enumerate}

\spaceSolution{3in}{}% Cool problem---if you use l'H\^{o}pital's rule, after the second iteration you can rewrite the limit of the sum as the sum of a limit; evaluating each gives 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/2, as expected. If you insist on continuing l'H\^{o}pital iterations, two more iterations will give you the same result. Taylor series make this limit quite easy to evaluate.
