\section{Exercise}

(2 pt) Consider the function $f: \reals^{2} \rightarrow \reals$ given by
\begin{align*}
f(x,y)
=
x^{4} y^{2} - 2 x y^{5}.
\end{align*}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item (1 pt) Compute $\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}$ and $\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}$.
\end{enumerate}

\spaceSolution{.75in}{% Begin solution.
\paragraph{Solution:}

Partial derivatives are computed by holding all variables but one constant:
\begin{align*}
\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}
&=
4 x^{3} y^{2} - 2 y^{5},
&
\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}
&=
2 x^{4} y - 10 x y^{4}.
\end{align*}
}% End solution.

\begin{enumerate}[resume,label=(\alph*)]
\item (1 pt) Compute $\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial y \partial x}$ and $\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x \partial y}$. Verify that the Clairaut--Schwarz theorem (equality of mixed partials) holds.
\end{enumerate}

\spaceSolution{1.5in}{% Begin solution.
\paragraph{Solution:}

We compute
\begin{align*}
\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial y \partial x}
=
\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right)
=
8 x^{3} y - 10 y^{4}
=
\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\right)
=
\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x \partial y}.
\end{align*}
These two second-order mixed partial derivatives are equal, as asserted by the Clairaut--Schwarz theorem. (Note that these second-order mixed partial derivatives are polynomials and therefore continuous everywhere, so the hypothesis of the theorem is satisfied.)
}% End solution.
