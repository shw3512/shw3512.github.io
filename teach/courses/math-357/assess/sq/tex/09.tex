\noindent{}Let $C_{4} = \langle{}g \st g^{4} = 1\rangle{}$ be the cyclic group of order $4$ with generator $g$. For each of the following maps, state whether it is a matrix representation of $C_{4}$, and if so, whether it is faithful.
\begin{align*}
\rho_{1}
:
C_{4}
&\rightarrow
\GL_{1}(\C)
&
\rho_{2}
:
C_{4}
&\rightarrow
\GL_{2}(\C)
&
\rho_{3}
:
C_{4}
&\rightarrow
\GL_{3}(\C)
\\
g^{j}
&\mapsto
\begin{pmatrix}
1
\end{pmatrix}
&
g^{j}
&\mapsto
\begin{pmatrix}
0	&	1	\\
-1	&	0
\end{pmatrix}%
^{j}
&
g^{j}
&\mapsto
\begin{pmatrix}
1	&	0	&	0	\\
0	&	-1	&	0	\\
0	&	0	&	i
\end{pmatrix}%
^{j}
\end{align*}

\spaceSolution{2in}{% Begin solution.
By definition, a representation is a group homomorphism. For our present examples, it suffices to check that $\rho(g^{4})$ is the identity matrix (why?). It is straightforward to check that this is true for all three maps. By definition, a representation is faithful if it is injective. The representation $\rho_{1}$ is not faithful, whereas $\rho_{2}$ and $\rho_{3}$ are.}% End solution.
